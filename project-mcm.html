Overview

This project addresses the 2026 MCM/ICM Problem D: developing a quantitative framework for NBA franchise valuation and strategic decision-making. We engineered a multi-level feature system from raw NBA historical data, built predictive models for team performance and revenue, and designed a Monte Carlo simulation engine to optimize roster management, load management, and ticketing strategies under luxury tax constraints.

My Role: Modeling lead. I was responsible for feature engineering, model selection, validation strategy design, and the Monte Carlo simulation framework for strategy optimization.

Key Contributions

Feature Engineering & Data Pipeline

  • Engineered a multi-level feature system from raw NBA data sourced from Basketball-Reference (2021–2025), quantifying player injury risks and team structural variance.
  • Constructed features to evaluate both competitive and commercial potential of NBA franchises, including player performance metrics, roster stability indices, and financial indicators.
  • Implemented strict temporal validation protocols to prevent data leakage in time-series sports prediction.

Predictive Modeling

  • Developed a cascading Gaussian Process Regression (GPR) pipeline to predict win rates, playoff progression depth, and franchise revenue from engineered features.
  • Utilized a custom multi-horizon validation strategy that strictly eliminates temporal data leakage by training on past seasons and testing on future seasons.
  • Compared GPR against Random Forest baseline models; GPR showed superior uncertainty quantification for sparse injury-risk scenarios.
  • Conducted hyperparameter optimization via grid search with walk-forward cross-validation.

Monte Carlo Strategy Optimization

  • Designed a Monte Carlo simulation engine integrating NBA luxury tax constraints and price elasticity models.
  • Optimized a complex decision space across roster trades, player load management, and dynamic ticketing pricing to maximize long-term franchise ROI.
  • Simulated multiple season trajectories under varying roster configurations to identify Pareto-optimal trade-offs between short-term competitiveness and long-term financial sustainability.

Model Architecture

Cascading GPR Pipeline: Stage 1 predicts regular-season win rate from roster features; Stage 2 takes win rate + team features to predict playoff depth; Stage 3 predicts franchise revenue from competitive outcomes. Each stage feeds into the Monte Carlo simulation for strategy evaluation.

主要贡献

特征工程与数据流程

  • 从Basketball-Reference（2021–2025）的NBA原始数据中构建多层次特征系统，量化球员伤病风险和球队结构差异。
  • 构建特征以评估NBA球队的竞技潜力和商业潜力，包括球员表现指标、阵容稳定性指数和财务指标。
  • 实施严格的时间序列验证协议，防止体育预测中的数据泄露。

预测建模

  • 开发了级联高斯过程回归（GPR）流程，从工程特征中预测胜率、季后赛深度和球队收入。
  • 采用自定义多 horizon 验证策略，通过过去赛季训练、未来赛季测试，严格消除时间数据泄露。
  • 将GPR与随机森林基线模型对比；GPR在稀疏伤病风险场景中表现出更优的不确定性量化能力。
  • 通过前向交叉验证的网格搜索进行超参数优化。

蒙特卡洛策略优化

  • 设计了整合NBA奢侈税约束和价格弹性模型的蒙特卡洛模拟引擎。
  • 在阵容交易、球员负荷管理和动态票务定价的复杂决策空间中进行优化，最大化长期球队投资回报。
  • 在不同阵容配置下模拟多个赛季轨迹，识别短期竞争力与长期财务可持续性之间的帕累托最优权衡。

模型架构

级联GPR流程：第一阶段从阵容特征预测常规赛胜率；第二阶段用胜率+球队特征预测季后赛深度；第三阶段用竞技结果预测球队收入。每个阶段的结果输入蒙特卡洛模拟进行策略评估。
